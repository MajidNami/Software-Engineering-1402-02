Present Continuous Tense

Explanation

The present continuous tense is used to describe actions that are happening at the moment of speaking or temporary actions. It can also be used to describe future plans or arrangements.

Structure

The structure of the present continuous tense is as follows:

  • Positive sentences: Subject + am/is/are + verb+ing
  • Negative sentences: Subject + am/is/are not (isn't/aren't) + verb+ing
  • Questions: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb+ing?

Examples

Positive sentences:

I am reading a book right now.

She is watching a movie at the moment.

Negative sentences:

He isn't (is not) working today.

We aren't (are not) going to the party.

Questions:

Are you studying for the exam?

Is she coming to the meeting?
