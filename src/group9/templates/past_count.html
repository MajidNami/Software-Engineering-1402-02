Past Continuous Tense

Explanation

The past continuous tense is used to describe actions or events that were in progress at a specific point in the past. It can also be used to set the scene in a story or describe a background action when something else happened.

Structure

The structure of the past continuous tense is as follows:

  • Positive sentences: Subject + was/were + verb+ing
  • Negative sentences: Subject + was/were not (wasn't/weren't) + verb+ing
  • Questions: Was/Were + subject + verb+ing?

Examples

Positive sentences:

I was reading a book when you called.

They were playing soccer at 6 PM yesterday.

Negative sentences:

She wasn't (was not) watching TV when I arrived.

We weren't (were not) sleeping at that time.

Questions:

Was he working late last night?

Were you studying when the power went out?
