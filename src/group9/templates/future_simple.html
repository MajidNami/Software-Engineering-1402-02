Future Simple Tense

Explanation

The future simple tense is used to describe actions or events that will happen in the future. It is often used to make predictions, promises, offers, and decisions made at the moment of speaking.

Structure

The structure of the future simple tense is as follows:

  • Positive sentences: Subject + will + base form of the verb
  • Negative sentences: Subject + will not (won't) + base form of the verb
  • Questions: Will + subject + base form of the verb?

Examples

Positive sentences:

I will call you tomorrow.

She will visit her grandparents next week.

Negative sentences:

He will not (won't) go to the party.

We will not (won't) finish the project on time.

Questions:

Will you help me with my homework?

Will they arrive by noon?
