Present Simple Tense

Explanation

The present simple tense is used to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements. It is one of the most commonly used tenses in English.

Structure

The structure of the present simple tense is quite straightforward:

  • Positive sentences: Subject + base form of the verb (add 's' or 'es' for third person singular)
  • Negative sentences: Subject + do/does not + base form of the verb
  • Questions: Do/does + subject + base form of the verb?

Examples

Positive sentences:

I play soccer every weekend.

She loves chocolate.

Negative sentences:

I do not (don't) like mushrooms.

He does not (doesn't) watch TV often.

Questions:

Do you play an instrument?

Does she work here?
